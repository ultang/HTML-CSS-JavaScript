Frequently asked questions - Click on the question below to see our advice

How do I get to Moore's Bed & Breakfast?

Full directions to Moore's can be found on our Getting Here page.

What are the check in times?

The regular check-in times are daily from 12:00pm – 6pm. If you plan to arrive outside of these hours please make the B&B aware by Contacting Us Here beforehand to arrange.

When is breakfast served?

Breakfast is served from 7.30am weekdays and 7am weekends. Full details on our breakfast can be found on our Menu page.

Can I book a smoking room?

No, all our rooms are non-smoking due to a smoking-ban in workplaces in Ireland, but you are weelcome to smoke outside of the B&B.

What is the weather in Galway like?

July is the hottest month in Galway with an average temperature of 16°C (60°F) and the coldest is February at 6°C (43°F) with the most daily sunshine hours at 8 in June. The wettest month is October with an average of 95.9mm of rain. The best month to swim in the sea is in August when the average sea temperature is 16°C (61°F). Realtime weather updates can be found Here

Are you certified by B&B Ireland?

Yes and we have also won TripAdvisor Certificate of Excellence in 2015 and 2016.

Are you suitable for small children and babies?

Yes we are child friendly and cots are available for babies.

Are you suitable for wheelchair users?

Yes we are wheelchair accessablie.

I have an allergy or dietry restriction, what can I have for breakfast?

We cater for any dietry resttrictions and allergies. Full details on our breakfast can be found on our Menu page.

Are you suitable for Guide Dogs and Pets?

In the interest of hygiene only Guide Dogs are allowed inside the premises. If you will be accompanied by a Guide Dog please make the B&B aware by Contacting Us Here beforehand to arrange.

What is the currency in Ireland?

Galway, the surrounding area of Connacht and the rest of the Republic of Ireland uses the Euro. Northern Ireland uses the pound Sterling. Some areas near the border accept both currencies.

What is the language in Ireland?

Most people speak English as their first language but Irish is also spoken in the Galway Gaeltacht area, the single largest and most populous Gaeltacht area in the country.
